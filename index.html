The Problem

You've Tried Tricks.
They Didn't Work. Here's Why.

"Guess the word from the picture." "Sound it out — sort of." "It'll click eventually."

These aren't systems. They're guesses dressed up as methods. Functional illiteracy is not a failure of intelligence — it is the silent consequence of being handed a map with half the roads missing.

Reading is a code. Like any code, it can be broken down, taught step by step, and mastered by anyone — but only if someone teaches the actual code, not a shortcut around it.

The Programme

Four Phases. One Complete Journey.

Every skill builds on the last. Nothing is introduced before the learner is ready.

Phase 01 · Chapters 1–5

Sound Foundations

Alphabet, vowels, consonants, short vowels, long vowels, and 220 sight words. Even if you already read — do you know why words sound the way they do? This phase fills every gap you never knew you had.

Phase 02 · Chapters 6–10

Cracking the Code

Consonant blends, digraphs, phonograms, the six syllable types, and silent letter patterns. If you have ever mispronounced a word, stumbled on an unfamiliar one, or guessed instead of decoded — this phase is the fix.

Phase 03 · Chapters 11–15

Architecture of Meaning

Morphemes, parts of speech, sentence mechanics, punctuation, and paragraph structure. Confident readers stop here and realise: they have been reading words — but not fully understanding the architecture holding them together.

Phase 04 · Chapters 16–19

Achieving Fluency

Comprehension at five levels, prosody, vocabulary development, and critical reading. The difference between reading and truly understanding what you read. This is where literacy becomes a superpower — for anyone.

The Method

Why This Works When Others Don't

⟶

Controlled Blending

Every word decoded one sound at a time — left to right, no guessing. Builds the neural pathway that makes fluent reading automatic.

◉

The Chin Test

A physical technique for counting syllables. Your own body becomes a reliable learning tool — no abstract rules to memorise.

✓

Controlled Texts

Practice passages use only words containing sounds already taught. Every exercise is designed to be completed successfully.
